Genealogically, OCaml comes from the line of programming languages whose grandfather is Lisp and includes other modern languages such as Clojure, F#, Haskell, and Racket.

OCaml originates from work done by Robin Milner and others at the Edinburgh Laboratory for Computer Science in Scotland. They were working on theorem provers in the late 1970s and early 1980s. Traditionally, theorem provers were implemented in languages such as Lisp. The type system of ML was carefully constructed so that you could only construct valid proofs in the language. A theorem prover was then written as a program that constructed a proof. Microsoft introduced its own variant of OCaml called F# in 2005.
